Share with Salem

A few months ago I found myself walking in the footsteps of Salem, a 13 year old Ethopian girl whose life was challenged by the global water crisis. I wasn't standing in Ethopia, I was standing in a mall in downtown New York City with a Samsung VR headset strapped on to my face. During the 9 minute film I emapthized with Salem; I found myself reaching out as if she was next to me. I wanted to turn around and hand Salem a bottle of water and share it with her. The feeling to help Salem and the barrier between us lingered long after taking off the VR headset.

How might I actually share water with Salem?

A Dash for A Drink

Amazon released a impressive IoT device called a Amazon Dash Button. The button is designed to be placed next to the thing you need, like paper towels. Everytime you need paper towels you push the button and Amazon will deliver paper towels in two days and you can continue living your life. It is cool technology but less than impressive value add to society. Who needs that many paper towels?

If I can press a button and make anything arrive at my doorstep in NYC why can't I press a button and make clean drinking water arrive for Salem in Ethopia?

How it Works

I repurposed an Amazon AWS Amazon Dash Button and stuck it by my sink. Everytime I'm grabbing a drink and feel like sharing one with someone like Salem I hit the button. At the end of the month I'm prompted with an email suggesting how much I should donate. The donation goes to Charity: water - a charity that does just about everything right and is the most effective channel in getting water to those in need.

The Dash button recieves three different 'donations'- a glass water, a pot of water, or a shower. Each setting a defined a different donation amount. I've also added an IFTTT logger app on my phone so I can share water on the go.

Tracker

I have a dashboard set up to keep an eye on how my shares are going. I log three different 'types' fo shares-- drinks, cooking, and showers. Each type of share has a different corresponding donation level which I'm still tweaking. A better insight into the cost to provide Salem a drinking water/cooking water/bathing water would lend credibility to the donations and is something I'm looking into further. Another benefit of all the Donation Dash is it interestign to see how much water I'm consuming in a day. "Hydration is half the battle" -My High School Coach.
